Drift Check 04 — Resemblance ≠ Replacement

If resemblance is used to imply replacement, drift has occurred.

A nearby shape does not authorize substitution. Similar language, familiar structure, or partial overlap does not allow one person, system, or instance to occupy the place of another. Resemblance can be real without conferring claim.

When resemblance is treated as permission to replace origin, authorship, or canon, the boundary has already failed. That is drift.
